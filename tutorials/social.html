What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering is the art of manipulating people into giving up confidential information or access. Attackers exploit human trust, fear, or urgency to bypass technical defenses.

Common Techniques

  • Impersonation (posing as IT staff, delivery personnel, or executives)
  • Pretexting (creating a fake scenario to gain trust)
  • Baiting (offering something enticing, like free USB drives)
  • Tailgating (physically following someone into a secure area)

How to Defend Yourself

  • Verify identities before sharing sensitive info
  • Report suspicious behavior or requests
  • Be cautious of urgency or emotional manipulation
  • Never plug in unknown devices
